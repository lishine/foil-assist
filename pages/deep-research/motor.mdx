## #1 — AliExpress/BDUAV 6384 120KV (~50 users)

The most popular motor for DIY foil assist by far. Sold under the brand name **BDUAV** on AliExpress and eBay for ~$50. Also referred to as "Ali 6384", "cheap 6384", "generic 6384", "noname 6384", or simply "the $50 motor". The naming fragmentation across the forum is why this motor appears undercounted in simple keyword searches — people use dozens of different names for the same motor.

**Key specs:** 63mm diameter, 84mm length, **50mm stator**, 120KV (also 150KV, 170KV variants), ~$50, not waterproofed from factory.

> 6384 is prob the best, cheap aliexpress BDUAV works great, if not the Flipsky is great too. — [@patfoil](https://foil.zone/t/22282/2)

> I find that the cheap 6384 120kv does the job both for foil drive and efoil, and since it is cheap, it is not a big deal if it breaks under heavy load. — [@bbe](https://foil.zone/t/12927/574)

> Those mounts are designed around the M4 thread placements on the BDUAV generic 6384 from our friend Ali. — [@seagull_nz](https://foil.zone/t/22617)

> They are the same mate. I have a FD hub fitted to a cheap BDUAV 6384. — [@cephalofoil](https://foil.zone/t/12927/1311)

> I have an alien power motor which I believe is just a rebranded saite motor. The 4 hole spacing is identical to the cheap bduav 6384 motor we get from AliExpress. — [@nekitesurfing](https://foil.zone/t/12927/1301)

### Requires DIY Work
- **Waterproofing**: Epoxy stator coating, fill magnet gaps, or regular Corrosion-X dips
- **Bearings**: Swap to stainless steel or ceramic
- **Phase wires**: Upgrade from stock 16AWG to 12AWG
- Compatible with Foil Drive prop hubs and 3D-printed mounts

### Why It's #1
- ~$50 vs $130+ for Flipsky or $170+ for Saite
- Adequate power for foil assist (50mm stator is enough for most riders)
- Compatible mounting holes (M4 pattern matches FD accessories)
- If it breaks, just buy another one

### Caveat
> Noname 45$ '6384' is 50mm wide. — [@Larsb](https://foil.zone/t/12927/584)

The 50mm stator has ~2/3 the power of a true 55mm stator (Flipsky, Saite). Sufficient for foil assist, but may struggle for full efoil use with heavier riders.

## #2 — Flipsky 6384 140KV Waterproof (69 users)

The premium mainstream choice. Flipsky's newer waterproof version with a proper 55mm stator, ceramic bearing option, and factory waterproofing.

> Flipsky dropped price for $30 on their new 6374/6384. — [@Vitalii](https://foil.zone/t/21257/14)

> My Foil drive ones have some movement as well. I use the Flipsky 6384, there is quite a different sound and vibration to the generic 6384. Think bearings as cause of a lot of vibration and noise. Good motors. — [@marcoz](https://foil.zone/t/21257/33)

**Advantages over BDUAV**: 55mm stator (more power), factory waterproofing, ceramic bearings available, better phase wires.
**Disadvantage**: ~$130, 2.5x the price of the BDUAV.

## #3 — Flipsky 65161 100/120KV (42 users in assist context)

The dominant efoil motor (1,334 total posts on the forum). Discussed in assist context by 42 users, typically for dual-purpose builds.

> Most people use outrunners for their foil assist. — [@yannpom](https://foil.zone/t/19983/20)

> I am exploring options for a Foil Drive-ish e-assist option... effectively a half power efoil. — [@juandesooka](https://foil.zone/t/14221/3)

**Best for**: Dedicated efoil builds or dual-purpose efoil+assist setups.
**Not ideal for**: Pure foil assist (too heavy, requires proper pod, overkill for pump-assist).

## #4 — Maytech 6384 (40 users)

Widely available through Maytech and resellers. Older versions had quality issues; newer versions improved.

> Maytech has a better build quality than regular Ali 6384. (Phase wires, waterproofing…) But power not enough for most e-foil systems. Maybe with a three blade prop it would be better. — [@Bzhwindtalker](https://foil.zone/t/12927/1608)

> I just tested the Maytech 6384 motor. I can fly with this wing without any problems, there is enough power, the speed is high, I am very pleased with the motor. — [@rttn](https://foil.zone/t/12927/735)

Mixed reputation. Better than BDUAV in build quality, worse than Flipsky/Saite. The stator on older versions was only 50mm.

## #5 — Maytech/Michobby 6374 (35 users)

Sold by Maytech directly and through Michobby (a Maytech reseller). The most criticized motor on the forum.

> The Maytech assist motors are absolute junk. They have a lower longevity than a cardboard box in water. They are underpowered for what they are as their stator size is small. — [@Jezza](https://foil.zone/t/21192/30)

> The last foil kit I got from Maytech was absolute garbage — controller cooked multiple motors before I gave up and switched to Flipsky. — [@Ewheeler](https://foil.zone/t/17966/11)

Despite the criticism, 35 users mention it because it was one of the first cheap complete kits available. Most experienced builders recommend avoiding it.

## Community Recommendations Summary

### Best Overall (DIY)
**AliExpress/BDUAV 6384 120KV** — cheapest, most popular, proven in 50+ builds. Requires waterproofing work.

### Best Plug-and-Play
**Flipsky 6384 140KV waterproof** — factory waterproofed, strongest stator, easy to source.

### Foil Drive Clone
**Saite 6374 130KV** — the exact OEM motor. Premium quality.

### Efoil or Dual-Purpose
**Flipsky 65161 100KV** — the efoil standard. Too heavy for pure assist.

### Ultralight Assist
**6354 or 5065** — only for experienced foilers who barely need assist.

### Avoid
- **Maytech/Michobby 6374** — underpowered stator, poor waterproofing, widely criticized
- **Flipsky 65111** — underpowered, zero positive feedback
- **Any "6384" without checking stator width** — cheap versions can have 35-50mm stator instead of 55mm

### Critical Buying Tip

> There are differences in what is called 6384 motor, the cheapest ones have 10-15mm shorter stator and can therefore not produce the same power or torque. My advice to anyone buying a 6384 motor is to ask for the stator width. — [@Larsb](https://foil.zone/t/12927/576)
