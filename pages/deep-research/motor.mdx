## #1 — 6384 Outrunner (143 users, 499 mentions)

The dominant motor for DIY foil assist. ~20% more torque than the 6374 due to longer stator.

### Manufacturer Breakdown

| Source | Assist Posts | Users | Notes |
|--------|------------:|------:|-------|
| **Flipsky** | 125 | 69 | Most popular. New waterproof version 140KV, 55mm stator. Ceramic bearings available |
| **Maytech** | 90 | 40 | Older version criticized for short stator (50mm), weak waterproofing. Newer versions improved |
| **Saite** | 46 | 23 | Foil Drive OEM factory. High quality. Filled with thermal epoxy |
| **APS (Alien Power System)** | 12 | 9 | 100KV version praised as "ideal for foil assist" |
| **BDUAV** | 12 | 9 | Budget Alibaba brand. 50mm stator (underpowered) |
| **Generic Ali/AliExpress** | — | ~20+ | Cheapest. Stator length varies wildly 35-55mm |

### Key Community Insights

> It's basically the same motor \[as 6374\], just in two different lengths of stator, and the stator length decides the max power and torque. This means 6384 is about 20% more motor than a 6374. — [@Larsb](https://foil.zone/t/21257/3)

> There are differences in what is called 6384 motor, the cheapest ones have 10-15mm shorter stator and can therefore not produce the same power or torque. I don't think 2/3 of a 6384 is enough to get up so that really matters. My advice to anyone buying a 6384 motor is to ask for the stator width. — [@Larsb](https://foil.zone/t/12927/576)

> The impregnated APS 6384 100 kV motor is absolutely ideal for foil drive assist, e-sup drive, motorized paddle etc — low cost, easy impregnation of a disassembled motor. — [@Dynamik](https://foil.zone/t/14221/7)

> I find that the cheap 6384 120kv does the job both for foil drive and efoil, and since it is cheap, it is not a big deal if it breaks under heavy load. — [@bbe](https://foil.zone/t/12927/574)

Common KV ratings: **120KV** (most common, 8-12S), **140KV** (good for 6-8S), **100KV** (APS, high torque), **150KV** (some builds), **170KV** (tested by a few for 6S)

### Positive Highlights
- Best power-to-weight ratio for foil assist
- Widely available, cheap, easy to source
- Fits standard Foil Drive prop hubs and 3D-printed mounts
- Proven in hundreds of builds across all conditions

### Negative Highlights
- Not all "6384" motors are equal — stator length varies 35-55mm depending on manufacturer
- Cheap Ali versions may have inadequate waterproofing
- Heavier than 6374 (matters for ultralight builds)
- Some Maytech versions have weak phase wires and poor QC

### Positive Highlights
- Proven design — the Foil Drive standard
- Lighter than 6384 (good for weight-conscious builds)
- Adequate for lighter riders and pure assist use

### Negative Highlights
- Less torque margin than 6384 — can struggle with heavier riders
- Maytech/Michobby versions widely criticized (short stator, bad waterproofing)
- Most community members now recommend upgrading to 6384 instead

## #3 — 65161 Inrunner (67 users in assist context, 102 mentions)

Primarily an efoil motor (1,334 total posts across forum), but discussed by 67 users in foil assist context.

### Source
Essentially **Flipsky only** — 52 assist posts, 42 users. Available in 100KV and 120KV.

### Key Community Insights

> It's half the torque of its big brother 65161. So it's not super strong and might work well for a foil assist setup. The real question is: isn't this motor too heavy for a light foil assist system? Most people use outrunners for their foil assist. — [@yannpom](https://foil.zone/t/19983/20)

> I am exploring options for a Foil Drive-ish e-assist option... effectively a half power efoil. — [@juandesooka](https://foil.zone/t/14221/3)

### When It Makes Sense
- Dual-purpose builds (efoil + assist on same board)
- Heavier riders who need maximum thrust
- Builds with dedicated pod/nacelle on the mast

### Why Most Avoid It for Pure Assist
- Too heavy for a lightweight bolt-on assist
- Requires a proper pod (not a simple mast clamp)
- Overkill for pump-assist use case

## Community Recommendations Summary

### For Most Builders
**Flipsky 6384 140KV waterproof** — best balance of power, availability, price, and community support.

### To Replicate Foil Drive
**Saite 6374 130KV waterproof** — the actual OEM motor used in Foil Drive Gen 2.

### Budget / Easy Kit
**Flipsky 6374 140KV** — adequate for lighter riders (&lt;80kg) with good pump technique.

### Lightweight / Minimalist
**6354 or 5065** — only for experienced foilers who barely need assist, prioritizing low weight.

### Avoid
- **Maytech 6374** — underpowered stator, poor waterproofing, widely criticized
- **65111** — underpowered for assist, zero positive feedback
- **Generic Ali 6384 without checking stator width** — can have 35-50mm stator instead of proper 55mm

### Critical Buying Tip

> Not all "6384" motors are equal. Always ask the seller for the actual stator width before purchasing. A cheap "6384" with a 50mm stator has roughly 2/3 the power of a proper 55mm stator version.
